Hi there! I'm Lyhorng — the person quietly (and sometimes not-so-quietly) deciding whether your Grab ride costs a little more on a rainy Friday evening. Sorry. Also, you're welcome.

As an Marketplace Mobility at Grab, I cover Cambodia 🇰🇭 and Myanmar 🇲🇲 — two frontier markets where every pricing decision is basically a live experiment with real stakes.

My days involve interrogating dashboards at 9am, simulating fare cuts over lunch, and convincing stakeholders that a 10% price drop is actually a great idea — with receipts. Spoiler: session growth went up 9%. The data never lies (but it does occasionally ghost you until 11pm).

Off-duty, I'm a professional overthinker (no, seriously — I will simulate 5 scenarios before deciding what to eat for lunch). I also play badminton, 3 tournaments deep, 1 silver medal to show for it — which I'm convinced happened because my opponent took one look at my game face and decided second place wasn't worth the trauma. Some call it toxic. I call it competitive pricing strategy, applied to sports.

EDLP — Right Pricing Initiative

Executed an Everyday Low Price fare cut of 10% for 3-wheelers in Phnom Penh. Ran pre/post analysis to validate the decision and brought stakeholders along for the ride.

📈 +9% session growth
🇲🇲 Myanmar

Streak Incentive — Yangon Launch

Led the rollout of the Streak driver incentive program in Yangon to replace the weekly incentive scheme, improving driver engagement and reliability metrics.

🔥 30% driver adoption

DAX Pricing Experiment

Designed and ran a combined experiment widening allocation radius and tweaking DAX (driver) pricing simultaneously to improve fulfillment rate.

✅ +1.1pp fulfillment rate
